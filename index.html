Koopman Operator Methods in Structural Health Monitoring: A Systematic Review Towards Hybrid Physics–Data Frameworks

A. V. Alagbada, T. Lahmer

Applied Sciences (MDPI), Vol. 16, Issue 7, Article 3392

A PRISMA-guided review of 67 peer-reviewed studies (2010–2025) tracing Koopman-based methods from Dynamic Mode Decomposition and Extended DMD to applications across civil, mechanical, and aerospace systems, and mapping the path toward hybrid physics–data frameworks for vibration-based condition assessment.

PROFILE

About

I am a PhD candidate in Applied Mathematics at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar, supervised by Prof. Tom Lahmer at the Institute of Structural Mechanics. My doctoral research develops Koopman-operator learning and hybrid physics–data frameworks for structural health monitoring, supported by the DAAD Scholarship (2025) and the PTDF Scholarship (2023/2024 cohort).

Before Weimar, I spent more than a decade teaching mathematics at secondary and university level in Nigeria, and I completed the ALX Software Engineering Programme with a backend specialization. That combination shapes how I work: mathematical rigour first, then honest software engineering, then clear communication of both.

I care about opening doors for the next generation of African researchers and share the realities of the PhD journey along the way.
